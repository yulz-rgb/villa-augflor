Vacation Tips

Expert Tips for Your Riviera Vacation

What to pack, where locals eat, travel hacks, and how to avoid the obvious tourist mistakes on the Côte d'Azur.

What to Pack for the Riviera

Clothing

  • Lightweight linen (heat hits 32°C in August)
  • Light jacket or wrap (evenings cool down)
  • Comfortable walking shoes (cobbled villages)
  • Smart casual for dinner (no shorts at nice restaurants)
  • Swimwear (multiple sets if staying a week)
  • Sunglasses (glare off water is intense)

Sunscreen & Essentials

  • SPF 50+ sunscreen (Mediterranean UV is fierce)
  • Reusable water bottle (tap water is excellent)
  • Small day bag for markets
  • Phone charger (long days out)
  • Comfortable sandals for beachfront dining
  • Hat or cap (less fashionable but practical)

Travel Hacks for the Riviera

Money & Payments

  • Use ATMs in Nice: Withdraw cash once at the airport. French ATM fees are lower than currency exchange.
  • Card acceptance varies: Small village restaurants often cash-only. Always ask before ordering.
  • Avoid exchange booths: Terrible rates. Use your bank's ATM card instead.

Getting Around

  • Rent a car for 2–3 days: Pick up at Nice airport, explore villages, drop off. €35–50/day is reasonable.
  • Public transport is cheap: €1.50 bus ride to Antibes. 10-ticket carnet = €12. Worth it.
  • Train stations are central: Nice/Antibes/Cannes train stations are in old towns. No need for transfers.

Meal Timing & Dining

  • Markets close by 1pm: Marché Provençal, Cours Saleya. Go early or you'll find half the stalls shuttered.
  • Lunch is 12–2pm, dinner is 7:30–10pm: Arriving at 5:30pm for dinner = closed kitchen. Plan ahead.
  • Plat du jour (daily special): Best value meal of the day. Usually €12–16, huge portion, fresh.
  • Rosé is the Riviera standard: Local Bellet or Provence rosés cost €10–18 at restaurants. Better than imported wine.

10 Things Tourists Miss

  1. Haut-de-Cagnes at dusk: Medieval village 5 minutes away, zero crowds, best rosé on a square you'll ever have.
  2. Marché Provençal before 9:30am: After 10am it's mostly tourists buying souvenirs. Arrive at 9, get real food.
  3. La Pesquière, Cros-de-Cagnes: Locals' fish restaurant. Not on TripAdvisor. Book ahead.
  4. Gorges du Loup: Dramatic limestone gorges, 45 min away, 5% of tourists know it exists.
  5. Île Sainte-Honorat ferry from Cannes: Monastic island with vineyards. Bring a picnic. Magical.
  6. Cap d'Antibes coastal walk: Sentier du Littoral. Crystal water, hidden coves, shoulders burning by noon.
  7. Grasse perfume workshops: Fragonard and Galimard. Skip the museum, do the hands-on workshop (€18–25).
  8. Vence instead of Saint-Paul: Matisse Chapel is here, village is less crowded, better restaurants.
  9. Bellet white wine (local): Only produced in Nice hills. €12 bottle tastes like €30. Buy from cave coopérative.
  10. Sunrise at Promenade des Anglais: 6am, no crowds, sea is perfect. Bring coffee and a book.

Avoid These Tourist Traps

Beware

  • Restaurant on Croisette (Cannes) — €45 pasta, mediocre. Walk 2 blocks away.
  • Souvenir shops on Promenade — €25 for a €3 item. Just don't.
  • "Local" ice cream in Old Town — Often mass-produced. Look for artisanal (crème glacée maison).
  • Taxi from Cannes to Nice — €80–120. Train is €15 and faster.

Instead, Do This

  • Find restaurants 1–2 blocks from major tourist areas.
  • Buy souvenirs at supermarkets (€1–3 vs €25 shops).
  • Ask locals where to get ice cream. They know.
  • Use public transport. Train tickets are cheap, air-conditioned, faster.

Best Seasons to Visit

June & September ✓

Warm sea (22–24°C), fewer tourists, calmer roads. Best price-to-experience ratio. Hotels 30% cheaper than July–August.

July & August ✗

Peak crowds, 32°C heat, expensive. Roads are gridlocked. Only go if you must.

May ✓✓

Perfect weather (20–24°C), spring flowers, Film Festival. Book 3+ months ahead.

October–April

Mild (12–18°C), quietest. Villa Augflor usually closed. Occasional weekend escapes available.
