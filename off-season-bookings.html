Ask About Off-Season

Why off-season is worth considering.

✓ Fewer tourists

April and October are pleasant but not peak season. Beaches less crowded. Restaurants easier to book. Local life still happening.

✓ Better weather than you think

April: 15–18°C, sunny 60% of days. October: 15–17°C, sunny 65% of days. Pool isn't ideal but garden is beautiful.

✓ Flowers and gardens

April: spring blooms (jasmine, bougainvillea in early leaf). October: mild climate, evening scent lingers.

✓ Flexible dates & rates

Off-season is negotiable. Want 5 nights instead of 7? Lower rate possible? Direct booking allows discussion.

✓ Quieter experience

No summer crowds. Lana has time for personal attention. Wine estates less busy. Hiking better (cooler temperatures).

✓ Local flavor

Interact with actual residents, not summer tourists. Local restaurants full of locals. Authentic Riviera experience.

Off-season reality check.

  • Pool: Not heated in April/October. Cold water (14–16°C). Outdoor shower will be cold. Consider gym passes or nearby heated pools if swimming essential.
  • Weather: Sunny but cool. Rain possible 30% of days. Garden is still beautiful but not warm-lounging-all-day vibes.
  • Restaurants: Many close October–May. Open ones are worth it—authentic local dining.
  • Nightlife: Quiet. No beach clubs or summer events. Peaceful, not vibrant.
  • Availability: Not guaranteed. Villa closed to owner unless specific circumstances. Worth asking, but don't expect confirmation.

Perfect off-season scenarios.

April: Spring escape

Garden in transition (new growth). Mild temperatures. Fewer tourists than summer. Easter holidays if timing works. Hiking season begins.

October: Extended fall

Summer heat gone. Water still swimmable early October. Quieter beaches. Perfect for outdoor exploration. Local food season begins.

NOT recommended:

November–March. Too cold for outdoor activities. Pool completely non-functional. Limited restaurant options. Save your money for summer or wait for April/October.
