The architecture and interiors carry the same distinctive eye: thoughtfully styled bedrooms, a luminous open-plan living area, and terraces oriented for morning coffee, afternoon sunbathing, and starlit dinners alike.

A note on the layout: Villa Augflor has a proper, spacious ground floor with the main living spaces, kitchen, pool, and terraces. Area guide

Your Riviera playbook

Everything Lana recommends — from the best boulangerie 2 minutes away to the most dramatic canyon in Europe. Updated each season.

Haut-de-Cagnes
5 min · Medieval village

The medieval hilltop village on the other side of our hill is beyond imagination — cinematographic, calm, classified as a historical site since 1948. Narrow pebbled streets cool even in summer heat, thousands of flowers, and a Renaissance château with panoramic views over sea and mountains. Take a glass of cold wine at the square café and simply exist. Use the free navette shuttle (bus 44 from Square Bourdet) to avoid parking.

Saint-Paul-de-Vence
15 min · Must-see

On the hill next to the villa, this 15th-century walled village is one of the great "must-see" destinations of the entire region. Art galleries, boutiques, hidden cafés, flowers everywhere. The Fondation Maeght modern art museum — with Miró's labyrinth and Giacometti in the courtyard — is world-class. Arrive early in summer.

Nice
25 min · Capital of the Riviera

A city of extraordinary layers — the Baroque Vieux-Nice, the Promenade des Anglais, the Cours Saleya flower market, the Russian Orthodox Cathedral. Founded by the Greeks (originally "Nike" — victory), it has been Italian, French, and everything in between. The spirit of Nice is unlike any other French city. The Cours Saleya morning market is essential at least once.

Monaco & Monte-Carlo
50 min · Grand principality

A tiny city-state famed for glamour, the Casino, the Prince's Palace (once home to Princess Grace), and some of the most prestigious events in the world — the Formula One Grand Prix, the Yacht Show, Monte Carlo Rally. Monte Carlo district is drenched in sun, money, and extraordinary Mediterranean views.

Èze Village
35 min · Most dramatic view

Perched at 427 metres above the sea, Èze is the most vertiginous village on the Riviera. The Jardin Exotique at the summit has the best panoramic view on the entire coast. Visit midmorning before the tour groups arrive. Combine with a coastal drive along the Grande Corniche.

Antibes & Juan-les-Pins
25 min · Old town & port

The walled old town, the Picasso Museum inside a real medieval castle, the extraordinary Saturday Provençal market, and the best bouillabaisse on the coast. Juan-les-Pins, attached to Antibes, has a legendary jazz festival each July. The superyacht port is a spectacle on its own terms.

Cros-de-Cagnes
15 min · Local beach

Long sandy beach right in the village. Relaxed, local, with good seafood restaurants on the waterfront. Ideal for families — calm water and gentle entry. Best for a lazy half-day followed by lunch at La Pesquière.

Golfe-Juan
25 min · Sandy beach

One of the most beautiful sandy beaches on the Riviera — clear shallow water, less crowded than Nice or Cannes, and a genuinely local atmosphere. Napoleon landed here on his return from Elba.

Cap d'Antibes
25 min · Hidden coves

Rocky coves and crystal-clear water around the cape. Take the Sentier du Littoral coastal walking path for hidden swimming spots away from the crowds. One of the most scenic walks on the entire Côte d'Azur.

Plage de la Garoupe
27 min · Glamorous beach

Antibes' most celebrated beach — shallow, sandy, sheltered. Beach clubs with loungers or a public section for BYO towel. Consistently beautiful water.

Nice Promenade
25 min · Iconic pebble beach

The famous pebble beach of Nice along the Promenade des Anglais. Best early morning or sunset. Combine with a walk through Vieux-Nice and the Cours Saleya market.

Mala Beach, Cap d'Ail
40 min · Hidden gem

A beautiful small pebble cove accessible only by coastal path. One of the Riviera's true secrets. Pack a picnic, wear shoes you can walk in, and allow an hour to reach it.

Boulangeries near the villa
2–8 min · Daily essentials

Closest: Patisserie des Travails, 19 Chemin des Travails (walk 12 min / car 2 min), open 06:30–19:30. Lana's favourite: Bour Pâtisserie Boulangerie Traiteur, 94 Avenue des Alpes (car 8 min), open 06:15–20:00 daily. Exceptional croissants and pastries.

Restaurants in Haut-de-Cagnes
5 min · Ask Lana for current picks

Several excellent small restaurants are nestled in the medieval village directly above the villa. Lana keeps a curated, up-to-date list with booking tips — just ask on arrival. The setting alone at these restaurants — candlelit medieval lanes — is worth the short drive.

Marché Provençal, Antibes
25 min · Daily until 1pm

The best morning market on the Riviera. Local cheese, olives, socca, charcuterie, flowers, and lavender. Essential at least once. Arrive around 9am with an empty bag and no fixed plan.

Cours Saleya Market, Nice
25 min · Tues–Sun morning

Nice's legendary flower and food market. Try fresh socca (Niçoise chickpea pancake) from a street stall for breakfast. One of the most atmospheric markets in France.

La Pesquière, Cros-de-Cagnes
15 min · Seafront

Beloved local fish restaurant right on the beach. Book ahead. Order the dorade grillée and a bottle of local Bellet white wine. One of the best casual lunches near the villa.

Rosé Wine Estates
Varied · Guided tastings

Provence produces the world's finest rosé. Château Minuty and Château Saint-Maur are both within an hour. Wine tastings can be arranged through Lana, including at estates not open to general public.

Fondation Maeght
15 min · Saint-Paul

One of Europe's great modern art museums — a Miró labyrinth, Giacometti sculptures in the courtyard, Calder mobiles, and world-class exhibitions all summer. Budget a minimum of two hours.

Watersports & Sailing
Antibes · 25 min

Antibes is the superyacht capital of the world. Sailing, paddleboarding, kayaking, and boat hire all available from the port. Lana can arrange half-day boat rentals and sailing lessons.

Gorges du Loup
45 min · Canyon hike

Spectacular limestone gorges in the Alpes-Maritimes hinterland — refreshingly cool even in August. Combine with a visit to the village of Gourdon perched at the top. A perfect escape from coastal heat.

Nice Jazz Festival
25 min · July

One of the world's great jazz festivals, held every July in Parc Phoenix. Outdoor concerts under the stars with Nice as the backdrop. Book tickets well in advance.

Cycling the Riviera
From the villa

Hire road or e-bikes and follow the coastal cycle path towards Nice or Antibes. The velodrome circuit at Cros-de-Cagnes is good for families. Lana can organise bike hire delivery to the villa.

Tennis & Golf
Varied · 10–25 min

Several excellent tennis clubs and golf courses nearby. Lana can arrange court time and tee times including at private clubs near Mougins and Biot. The climate means outdoor courts are playable from March through October.

Îles de Lérins
Cannes ferry · 45 min total

Take the ferry from Cannes to Île Sainte-Honorat — a monastic island with extraordinary vineyards and deserted rocky coastline. Île Sainte-Marguerite has the cell where the Man in the Iron Mask was imprisoned. Pack a picnic and spend the day.

Grasse
40 min · Perfume capital

The world capital of perfume since the 17th century. Visit Fragonard or Galimard for a perfume creation workshop — one of the best activities for all ages on the Riviera. Surrounding lavender and mimosa fields are extraordinary.

Tourettes-sur-Loup & Gourdon
30–45 min · Inland villages

Tourettes-sur-Loup is known for its violet festival and artisan craft shops. Gourdon, perched on a cliff 760 metres above the sea, has the most breathtaking views of the entire arrière-pays. Both can be combined in a half-day loop.

Menton & the Italian Border
55 min · Lemon capital

The prettiest town on the Riviera, at the Italian frontier. Famous for lemons, Baroque churches, and the Ventimiglia market just across the border on Fridays. Excellent for an authentic half-day excursion away from the tourist trail.

Verdon Gorge
2 hrs · Europe's Grand Canyon

The most dramatic landscape in southern France — turquoise water, 700-metre limestone cliffs, and a canyon stretching 25 kilometres. Kayaking, rock climbing, or simply the most dramatic drive imaginable. A full-day adventure worth planning.

Vence & Matisse Chapel
20 min · Art pilgrimage

The Chapelle du Rosaire, designed by Henri Matisse in his final years, is a masterwork of light, ceramic, and stained glass. Quieter and more authentic than Saint-Paul. Local Tuesday market. One of the essential cultural stops of the region.

Supermarkets & everyday shopping
All within 5–8 min by car

Leclerc — 344 Chemin des Moulières, La Colle-sur-Loup. Open 08:30–20:30, Sunday 09:00–13:00 (walk 20 min / car 8 min). Also has the cheapest fuel station in the area.

Casino Supermarché — Avenue des Alpes. Open 06:00–midnight (walk 17 min / car 5 min). Very convenient for evening top-ups.

LIDL — 66 Avenue de Verdun. Open 08:30–20:00 (walk 17 min / car 5 min).

Polygone Riviera — 119 Avenue des Alpes. Open 10:00–20:00 (car 4 min). Large shopping centre with Zara, H&M, Apple Store, restaurants, and a good supermarket. The closest major retail destination to the villa.

Boulangeries
2–8 min

Patisserie des Travails, 19 Chemin des Travails — 2 min by car, 12 min walk. Open 06:30–19:30.

Bour Pâtisserie Boulangerie, 94 Ave des Alpes — 8 min by car. Open 06:15–20:00 daily. Lana's personal favourite for croissants.

Useful to know
Local tips

Free shuttle bus (navette gratuite, line 44) from Square Bourdet town centre runs every 20 minutes daily to Haut-de-Cagnes — avoids parking entirely.

Cheapest fuel: Leclerc station, La Colle-sur-Loup.

Pharmacy: Several in the town centre, open standard French hours. Nearest urgent care at Hôpital de Cagnes-sur-Mer.

We strongly recommend travel insurance for all bookings. It is the guest's responsibility to be aware of any restrictions that may impact their stay.

The pool is for the exclusive use of guests staying at the property. No glass in or around the pool at any time — plastic cups are provided.

Children must be supervised at all times in the pool area. The pool is deep.

Questions & answers

Everything you need to know

What is the minimum stay?+
A 7-night minimum applies for all open season months (June, July, August, September). Contact Lana directly if you're interested in a shorter stay — availability around existing bookings occasionally allows for it.
Are pets allowed?+
Unfortunately pets are not permitted at Villa Augflor. This applies to all animals including dogs and cats. The policy protects guests with allergies and preserves the garden and pool. We appreciate your understanding.
What are the bed sizes?+
The three bedrooms have beds sized 200×220 cm, 180×200 cm, and 160×200 cm respectively. Please let Lana know if you have specific preferences when booking and she'll do her best to allocate accordingly.
Tell me more about the layout — the loft upstairs.+
Villa Augflor has a full, spacious ground floor with the main living areas, kitchen, dining, and direct access to the pool and terraces. The upper floor is a loft-style space — characterful and cosy, but with lower ceilings and a steep staircase to reach it. Guests who need easier access or prefer standard ceiling heights may wish to request ground-floor rooms. Please ask Lana when booking.
How much does booking direct save?+
Booking directly through this website, by email, or via WhatsApp saves up to 20% compared to Airbnb and other platforms (which charge a 14–17% guest service fee). You also get direct communication with Lana, flexible payment terms, and access to concierge services not available through third parties.
Is there parking? How do we get from Nice Airport?+
There is private parking at the villa for 2 cars. Nice Côte d'Azur Airport is 15 minutes by car. Lana can arrange a private airport transfer (recommend booking in advance). Cagnes-sur-Mer train station — served by direct regional and TGV trains — is also very close to the villa.
Can we arrange a private chef or catering?+
Yes. Lana works with trusted local chefs and caterers who can prepare meals at the villa — from a romantic Provençal dinner for two to a full-group feast with local wine pairings. Mention it in your booking enquiry and Lana will handle the rest.
Is the villa available in April, May, or October?+
The villa is generally closed outside the main season (June–September). However, if you're interested in those months, contact Lana directly — individual room availability may occasionally exist. It's always worth enquiring.
